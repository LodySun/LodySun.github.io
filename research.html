Research

What I work on

MSc Thesis · University of Helsinki · supervised by Benjamin U. Cowley

Staged rule acquisition in WishGLD

Modeling rule learning as a temporally extended, multi-marker transition rather than a single event. A two-state hidden Markov model estimates trial-wise acquisition dynamics, compared against the first-correct response and a logical-collapse marker, and related to feedback-locked EEG (FRN, P3b) and inter-subject representational similarity (IS-RSA).

Task development

Four-Rule WCST

Designing and implementing a four-rule WCST in PsyToolkit (adding an orthogonal orientation rule to the classic dimensions) to support future reinforcement-learning modeling of multidimensional strategy use.

Undergraduate Thesis · CAS Institute of Neuroscience · supervised by Zheng Ye

Locus coeruleus integrity and set-shifting in Parkinson's disease

Quantified locus coeruleus / substantia nigra integrity from neuromelanin-MRI and linked these structural metrics to set-shifting deficits (CANTAB IED) in Parkinson's disease.

Future directions

Narrative & event memory

Toward mapping the microstructure of narrative and non-linear storytelling onto predictive neural dynamics: event segmentation, prediction error, and individual differences in how people build coherent event structure from continuous experience.
